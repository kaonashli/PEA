PEA Project

Learning Centre System

The Brigil Learning Centre (BLC) has been providing tutoring services to students in various subjects. Due to COVID19 and social distancing, teachers at the BLC had been meeting with students online through Discord. Although Discord is a great tool, it had some limitations. The BLC needed a system that could allow students to queue up for online or in-person meetings. Additionally they also needed a way to automatically log information about those meetings.
Our solution was to create the Learning Center System (LCS). Our system improved the overall experience of users by replacing the physical log book, reducing overall paperwork that professors must track, and by allowing students to queue up anytime during the professor's tutoring session. This system was developed by Ashley Boily, Ibrahim Ahmed, Jeremy Barbeau-Gagnon, Gabriel Jolicoeur-McCharles, Maxim Lam and Tyler Lehmann with Richard Chan as our project manager during the Fall 2020 and Winter 2021 semesters. The team worked together in 2 week sprints using Team Foundation Server for version control, to store documents and to manage our tasks.

Applied Experience

At the end of our first and second year, students have the opportunity to work 2 co-op work terms. This gives us the chance to gain experience before graduating. For each work term, students are required to have an average of 70% in all their CS courses as well as write a co-op report at the end of the work term.
